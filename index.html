Our Story

  • Mentorship app for Women Who Code
  • Fast-track your career prospects
  • Peer learning and coaching made simple
  • Build an enduring professional network
We all know that getting the right advice at the right time and from the right people can dramatically change your life.

In today's world, it can be daunting for anyone to navigate the career jungle. This is especially true for women in technology. The tech world can be a bit of a boy's club. There are many women out there who are passionate about technology, who want to advanced their careers in technology, but who aren't sure where to start. These women need all the help they can get.

One organization that is dedicated to promoting women in technology is Women Who Code. We created MentorMe for Women Who Code. We had the following in mind. First, we would like to see more women fast-track their careers through mentorship. To make this happen, we designed our app to make finding and requesting coaching from anyone super simple and fun. Lastly, we hope MentorMe will enable more women to build enduring professional networks.

Now, Nidhi hear will tell you more about our project.

Inspirations

We benchmarked many apps and two stood out the most. The first is Facebook Messenger, we really like its use of rounded profile image views. The second is an app from Google called One Today. It lets you discover non-profits every day and donate to projects you like. We love this app's fonts, layout, and user experience.

Now let's see how the app works for someone seeking mentorship. Nidhi here is an Android enthusiast who wants to know what it's like to be a professional Android developer.

UI features we may want to highlight (What sets us apart from other Mentoring apps?):

  • Women Who Code branding colors (action bar, fonts, pictures), Splash screen background is a slide show of images from Women Who Code meetups
  • Mentor list view dims as we slide out the navigation drawer
  • ListViewItems grow into view
  • Flip animation when switching between list view and map view
  • Action bar slides up and disappears to make room onscreen as we scroll down
  • Edit profile shake animation to indicate missing required fields
  • Edit profile action bar shows which step you're in.
  • Request to connect sends a push notification to the target user
  • Push notifications contain user's profile images
